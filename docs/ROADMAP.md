# Azur Menton Roadmap

This roadmap keeps the direct-booking objective first while allowing the Menton Guide to grow into a maintained local resource. Reaching a milestone triggers a review and recommendation; it never authorizes automatic publishing, event changes, partner placement, or booking changes.

## Current Baseline

- Three apartments, manual availability requests and booking-funnel tracking are live.
- Guide, places, map, event and stay-page content is typed and validated.
- The priority guide authority layer covers 20 maintained high-intent pages.
- The owner-only funnel dashboard is ready. It needs a local read-only Plausible Stats API key before it can select pages from live data.

## M1 - Strong Guide Base

**Outcome:** roughly 100 useful published guides, not a target reached through thin or duplicate pages.

**Exit conditions**

- 95-105 guides with a clear guest or search intent.
- Each new guide passes content, image, linking and map-review rules where it has fixed places.
- High-intent guides have apartment-aware links and, where maintained, an authority profile.
- `content:report` has no broken references, orphan content or unresolved coverage gaps.

**At the gate:** review the remaining intent gaps before creating more articles. Do not publish a guide only to reach 100.

## M2 - Living Downloadable Guide

**Outcome:** one curated downloadable Menton guide with a stable landing URL and controlled editions.

**Exit conditions**

- A selected, maintained subset of guides has consistent editorial quality and rights-cleared media.
- The downloadable edition has a version number, publication date and update notes.
- The download page clearly explains that readers should return for the latest edition; an already-downloaded file does not change itself.
- The workflow can regenerate a new edition from reviewed content without duplicating facts by hand.

**At the gate:** prototype one English edition and owner-review its scope, attribution, privacy and distribution before adding translations or sales.

## M3 - Weekly Guide Maintenance Report

**Outcome:** a weekly, human-reviewed report that proposes guide maintenance work.

**Exit conditions**

- The report identifies source freshness, missing images, weak links, map-review backlog and possible guide ideas.
- Each recommendation includes evidence, affected URL and a proposed action.
- The report is proposal-only: no guide or source is changed automatically.

**At the gate:** enable a scheduled report and approve a small first maintenance batch manually.

## M4 - Event Intelligence Report

**Outcome:** a weekly editorial queue for Menton, Monaco, Nice and Sanremo events.

**Exit conditions**

- The report distinguishes confirmed official information, dates pending and ideas requiring research.
- It flags stale annual series, expiring confirmed dates, source gaps and likely booking relevance.
- Every proposed event includes a primary/official source and no event is auto-published.

**At the gate:** approve the source list and a review workflow before creating a detailed public events publication cadence.

## M5 - Paid Menton Books

**Outcome:** one or two focused, independently useful books sold through Amazon and linked from the site.

**Exit conditions**

- A clear reader problem and differentiated outline for each book.
- Rights, attribution, ISBN/tax/platform and update-policy decisions are documented.
- The manuscript is edited as a book, not exported wholesale from the website.
- A neutral site page links to the book without weakening apartment booking paths.

**At the gate:** validate a single book with a full sample, cover, metadata and proof copy before starting the second.

## M6 - Merchandise

**Outcome:** a small, optional shop built around rights-cleared original work or book-related products.

**Exit conditions**

- Product, fulfilment, returns, tax and customer-support ownership are defined.
- The shop is visually separate from booking conversion paths.
- No product imagery, artwork or claims are used without explicit rights.

**At the gate:** launch a narrow pilot rather than a broad catalogue.

## M7 - Local Partner Programme

**Outcome:** a selective guest-perk programme that improves stays without becoming an undisclosed advertising portal.

**Exit conditions**

- Enough guest volume exists to make a small trial meaningful.
- Free samples, guest-only perks, editorial mentions and paid/sponsored visibility remain explicitly separate.
- Product safety, storage, labelling, disclosure and partner review rules are operational.
- No partner appears publicly by default.

**At the gate:** run a small guest-only pilot with manual partner selection and review results before public sponsorship or marketplace work.

## Recurring Checkpoint

The weekly Codex reminder checks this roadmap against repository reports and, when configured, aggregate funnel data. It should report only:

1. which milestone gates are near or met;
2. the strongest evidence and unresolved dependencies;
3. one recommended next action.

It must not edit files, send messages, publish events, submit URLs, or change bookings without a separate explicit task.
